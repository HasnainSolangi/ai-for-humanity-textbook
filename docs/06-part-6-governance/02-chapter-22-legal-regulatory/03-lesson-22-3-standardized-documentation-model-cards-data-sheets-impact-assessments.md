# Standardized Documentation: Model Cards, Data Sheets, Impact Assessments

Documentation is the only proof that you did the work.

## Model Cards (The Resume)
Standardized by Google/Margaret Mitchell.
*   **Intended Use:** "This model is for translating French to English."
*   **Out of Scope:** "Do not use for medical translation."
*   **Metrics:** "Accuracy is 90% on Wikipedia, but 60% on Slang."

## Data Sheets for Datasets (The Ingredients)
Standardized by Timnit Gebru et al.
*   **Motivation:** Why was this dataset created?
*   **Composition:** Who is in it? (Age, Race, Gender breakdown).
*   **Collection Process:** Was it scraped? Was consent obtained?

## Algorithmic Impact Assessments (The Forecast)
Standardized by the Canadian Government.
*   **Question:** "What bad things could happen?"
*   **Answer:** A structured narrative identifying potential harms (bias, privacy, security) and the specific mitigations taken.

This documentation allows an auditor to travel back in time and understand the decisions made during development.
